# Product

## Register

product

## Users

D&D players (3-6 people) and their Dungeon Master, used during and between campaign sessions to track inventory, missions, lore, characters, locations, spells, and combat. Players check in on phones or laptops at the table. The DM uses admin tools to manage items, quests, and accounts.

## Product Purpose

A campaign companion app for The Legends of Essolis D&D 5e campaign. Replaces physical notes and spreadsheets with a living, themed reference tool. Success means players can find what they need quickly without breaking immersion, and the DM can manage content without friction.

## Brand Personality

Arcane and purposeful. Clear enough to use mid-session without confusion, atmospheric enough to feel like part of the world. "A spellbook that actually works." Not decorative clutter — weighted craft.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards: white cards, blue accents, Inter/system fonts, generic data UI
- Children's fantasy: bright primary colors, rounded bubble fonts, playful cartoonish tone
- Anything that feels like a spreadsheet wearing a costume

## Design Principles

1. **Legibility over decoration.** Every ornamental choice must survive mid-session use. If it slows reading, remove it.
2. **Atmosphere through restraint.** The medieval fantasy mood comes from typography, texture, and color — not from cramming every surface with ornaments.
3. **Hierarchy from space and weight.** Spacing and typographic contrast do the structural work; color accents signal action and status, not structure.
4. **Functional first.** Content layouts serve the task (find item, read lore, check mission) before they serve aesthetics.
5. **Consistent depth.** The dark palette creates depth through shadow and subtle gradient; don't flatten it with overlit surfaces.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No specific WCAG requirements stated. Target WCAG AA for contrast. Dark theme only (fits the ambient use context of gaming tables and dim rooms). Responsive for phone use at the table.
